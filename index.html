About Cyber Security

Cyber Security in online transactions refers to the technologies, processes, and practices designed to protect networks, devices, programs, and data from attack, damage, or unauthorized access during financial exchanges.

As digital banking and e-commerce grow exponentially, the importance of securing these transactions has never been higher. The key challenges involve staying ahead of sophisticated phishing schemes, securing legacy banking systems, and ensuring minimal friction for the end-user while maintaining robust authentication.

Common Cyber Threats

Phishing

Deceptive emails or messages designed to trick users into revealing sensitive credentials.

OTP Fraud

Scammers manipulate users into sharing their One-Time Passwords to authorize illicit transactions.

Malware & Spyware

Malicious software secretly installed to monitor and steal banking details from user devices.

Fake Payment Apps

Counterfeit applications that mimic legitimate banks to harvest login information.

Card Fraud

Skimming or cloning debit/credit cards to make unauthorized purchases online.

Security Measures

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Adding an extra layer of security beyond just passwords.

Biometric Authentication

Using unique biological traits like fingerprints or facial recognition.

Secure Payment Gateways

Encrypted channels that securely transmit transaction data.

AI Fraud Detection

Machine learning models that analyze transaction patterns in real-time.

Conclusion & Future Scope

The landscape of online transactions is continually evolving, and so are the threats that target them. Our study highlights that while traditional security measures like OTPs and passwords are necessary, they are no longer sufficient on their own.

Future Scope: Integrating advanced Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning algorithms directly into payment gateways will be critical. AI can dynamically assess risk factors in milliseconds, providing an invisible yet robust shield against cyber threats without disrupting the user experience.
